People over 65 qualify for Medicare beneifits instead of Affordable Care Act benefits.
Should I apply for subsidies anyway?
That depends. Special circumstances could place you into a qualifying category. Some examples are if anyone in your household has a disability, has ever been part of the foster care system, is of a Native American descent, or if your family is experiencing financial hardships that might not be obvious from your tax returns, among others. If you feel your family might qualify under special circumstances, you should definitely at least contact a State case manager and get some advice.
How much would it cost me to opt out?
1% of your Modified Adjusted Gross Income on your federal tax return, or $95, whichever is greater.
